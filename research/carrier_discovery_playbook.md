# Florida Carrier Discovery Playbook

Date: 2026-03-04

## Goal

Find more Florida-based carriers that operate their own:

- truck-mounted forklifts
- piggyback forklifts
- Moffett / Moffet / Moffett-style forklifts
- Princeton / Donkey truck-carried forklifts

This niche is hard to source because many real carriers do not advertise the capability clearly on their websites.

## What works best

### 1. Search for service language, not just equipment words

The best official-site signals are usually service phrases like:

- jobsite delivery
- off-road truck mounted forklift
- flatbed plus forklift
- onsite loading / offloading
- construction materials delivery
- overflow fleet support
- direct freight shipping company
- not a broker

Pure equipment keywords alone (`Moffett`, `piggyback forklift`) often miss good carriers because many companies describe outcomes instead of equipment.

### 2. Search by metro + service type

Florida carrier discovery works better by metro than statewide.

Priority metros:

- Miami / Hialeah / Doral / Medley
- West Palm Beach / Riviera Beach
- Orlando / Sanford / Kissimmee
- Tampa / Largo / Pinellas
- Fort Myers / Naples
- Jacksonville / Orange Park / Yulee
- Pensacola / Panhandle

Useful query patterns:

- `"<metro>" "jobsite delivery" forklift`
- `"<metro>" flatbed forklift delivery`
- `"<metro>" "truck mounted forklift"`
- `"<metro>" Moffett delivery`
- `"<metro>" piggyback forklift trucking`
- `"<metro>" "construction materials delivery" carrier`
- `"<metro>" "donkey lift" delivery`
- `"<metro>" "flatbed with Moffett"`
- `"<metro>" "onsite unloading" flatbed`
- `"<metro>" "direct carrier" flatbed forklift`

### 3. Use official dealer networks as indirect lead sources

Dealer pages do not identify carriers directly, but they show where truck-mounted forklift demand is strongest.

Example:

- [Ring Power Lift Trucks](https://lifttrucks.ringpower.com/buy/specialty-trucks/truck-mounted-forklifts) confirms Moffett demand corridors across North, Central and South Florida.
- [Princeton / PIGGY BACK dealer locator](https://www.piggy-back.com/locate-dealer/) shows Florida coverage through Briggs Equipment in Orlando, Jacksonville, Tampa and Miami.

That is useful because the best prospects to call manually are often:

- builders-supply delivery fleets
- lumber and truss distributors
- roofing distributors
- masonry / paver / block delivery operators
- pallet and industrial transport companies

What to do with dealer networks:

- map the Florida dealer coverage first
- build a metro list around each dealer territory
- search for local fleets using the dealer's product names (`Moffett`, `PiggyBack`, `Princeton`, `Donkey`)
- use the dealer territory map as a proxy for where active truck-mounted forklift fleets are most likely concentrated

### 3A. Search official fleet pages from suppliers and distributors

Some of the best evidence of truck-mounted forklift usage appears on shipper fleet pages, not carrier pages.

Example:

- [Mercedes Electric Supply fleet page](https://mercedeselectric.com/about-us/delivery-fleet-services/) shows a `30' flatbed w/ Donkey Lift` and a `5,000 lbs all-terrain forklift`.

These companies should not be counted as carriers by default, but they are still valuable because they can become:

- overflow fleet leads
- backup capacity for dedicated local lanes
- phone-verification targets to ask whether they ever haul third-party freight
- pattern matches for similar companies in the same vertical

### 3B. Use job ads and classifieds only to discover names, phones, and phrasing

Lead-gen only sources can expose the exact language operators use publicly:

- Craigslist ads
- CDL job posts
- labor / hauling listings

Useful examples from this pass:

- Craigslist ads in South Florida used phrases like `flatbed delivery services with piggyback forklift`, `same day`, `job sites`, and `licensed & insured`
- job posts often mention `Moffett driver`, `piggyback forklift`, or `job site unloading`

But these sources should only trigger the next step:

- find an official website
- match the business name or phone to a legal entity
- confirm they are transport-for-hire and not just a private fleet

### 4. Treat classifieds and social posts as lead-gen only

Craigslist, Facebook, OfferUp and truck-sale sites often expose operators who clearly advertise:

- piggyback forklift
- truck mounted forklift
- donkey
- Princeton
- Moffett

But those sources should never be imported directly into the master list.

Use them only to generate leads, then verify through one of:

- official company website
- FMCSA / SAFER
- direct phone confirmation
- active business listing that matches company identity

### 5. Separate three categories before adding a row

Every lead should be classified before entering the carrier file:

1. `verified`
   Official site clearly shows they are a transport carrier and confirms the forklift capability.

2. `partial`
   Official site confirms transport service and strongly implies or states onboard forklift capability, but legal/contact/compliance detail is thin.

3. `discard`
   Broker, 3PL, equipment dealer, rental-only company, or shipper fleet with no clear for-hire carrier signal.

## Best source hierarchy

Use this order:

1. Official carrier website
2. Official service page for a specific capability
3. Official contact / about / locations page
4. FMCSA / SAFER or other compliance confirmation
5. Classifieds / social / directories only as lead generation

If the only source is a marketplace, directory, or lead-form site, do not add the company to the master carrier list.

## Red flags

Do not count a lead just because:

- it has forklifts
- it has liftgates
- it sells pallets
- it is a building supplier with trucks
- it rents forklifts
- it offers only unloading labor
- it is a broker matching carriers

Additional red flags from this pass:

- location mismatch across pages
- generic or templated SEO site with thin company identity
- footer or contact data pointing to another state while the page claims Florida local operations
- no legal entity, no dispatcher phone, and no stable company identity
- site reads like a pallet seller or building supplier but not a for-hire carrier

Example of a likely red-flag pattern:

- pallet sites that claim forklifts, GPS tracking, statewide routes and COI support can look attractive, but if the contact page or footer points to another state or the site lacks clear company identity, treat it as `discard` until independently verified

The lead should show both:

- transport-for-hire or direct delivery service
- self-unload capability from its own truck/trailer setup

## Recommended next pass

The most likely places to find additional valid Florida carriers are:

1. South Florida pallet / building-material haulers
2. Jacksonville and Orange Park flatbed / construction delivery carriers
3. Panhandle construction material delivery fleets
4. Treasure Coast builder-supply support fleets
5. Overflow fleet vendors serving lumber, truss, roofing and masonry suppliers

The highest-yield workflow is:

1. pick one metro
2. search official sites using service-language queries
3. scan dealer territory clues for that metro
4. collect shipper private fleets with donkey / Moffett capability
5. phone-verify whether they accept outside loads or overflow hauling
6. only then upgrade them into the carrier list

## Florida-specific channels worth using next

- Ring Power Moffett territories for North/Central/South Florida
- Princeton / PIGGY BACK Florida dealer coverage through Briggs Equipment
- supplier fleet pages that explicitly mention `Donkey Lift`, `Moffett`, `truck-mounted forklift`, or `jobsite unloading`
- CDL driver ads that require Moffett or piggyback experience
- Sunbiz / FMCSA confirmation after a name or phone number is identified

## Recommended enrichment fields

For every candidate carrier, capture:

- legal / operating name
- city and region
- website
- phone
- email
- direct dispatch or sales contact
- explicit equipment signal
- service radius
- jobsite / construction fit
- MC / DOT verification status
- whether they accept brokered freight

## Honest current bottleneck

The main blocker is not lack of companies. It is lack of public self-description.

Many valid carriers likely exist in Florida, but they describe themselves with phrases like:

- final mile
- delivery support
- construction delivery
- fleet outsourcing
- dedicated transportation arm

instead of:

- Moffett
- piggyback forklift
- truck-mounted forklift

That means the highest-yield next step after web research is targeted phone verification by metro.
